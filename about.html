About Wavelength

A small, stubborn blog about how songs get made, why they sound the way they do, and who we become when we listen closely.

Made slowly, on purpose.

Wavelength started in a cramped bedroom studio in Brooklyn during the summer of 2023. The premise was simple: write about music the way musicians actually talk about it — not as critics, but as curious practitioners.

We publish long-form essays twice a month. No hot takes. No album reviews. Just patient, reported pieces on the craft of making and hearing sound. We've visited studios in Reykjavik, portable recording rigs in Tokyo apartments, and vinyl pressing plants in Detroit.

Every piece is edited for clarity, fact-checked, and read aloud at least once before it goes out. If it doesn't sound right, we rewrite it.

Whether you're a producer, a bedroom musician, or someone who just loves to put on headphones and disappear into a record — this is a space for you.

History

How we got here.

A short timeline of Wavelength's journey so far.

2023

Wavelength starts in a Brooklyn bedroom studio. First issue goes out to 37 subscribers — mostly friends and fellow producers.

2024

Crossed 1,000 subscribers. Published our first studio visit piece from Reykjavik. Hired our first contributing writer.

2025

3,000 subscribers. Visited 12 studios across 8 cities. Published "Why Fade-Outs Disappeared" — our most-read piece to date. Started paying all contributors.

2026

4,300+ subscribers. 37 studios visited across 14 cities. Expanded to include curated playlists and field recording diaries. Still small, still stubborn, still slow.
